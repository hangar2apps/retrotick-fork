## Production Guidelines - Competitive Focus

### Visual Strategy
- **Split screens**: Competitors struggling vs RetroTick succeeding
- **Timer comparisons**: Show speed differences dramatically
- **Error messages**: Highlight competitor pain points
- **Price callouts**: Make cost differences obvious

### Messaging Framework
- **Problem**: Competitor limitations/complexity
- **Agitation**: Show the frustration/cost/time waste
- **Solution**: RetroTick's simplicity
- **Proof**: Side-by-side demonstration
- **Call to Action**: Direct competitive choice

### Competitor Callouts
**Direct Mentions** (when legally safe):
- "Unlike VMware..." 
- "While DOSBox requires..."
- "Parallels charges $200 for what we do free..."

**Indirect References**:
- "Complex emulators"
- "Expensive VM software" 
- "Technical setup requirements"

### Distribution Strategy

**Competitive Platforms**:
- **YouTube**: Target competitor video comments
- **Reddit**: r/emulation, r/sysadmin (where people complain about alternatives)
- **Forums**: DOSBox forums, virtualization communities
- **Social**: Reply to complaints about VM software costs

**SEO Competitive Terms**:
- "Alternative to Parallels"
- "Free VMware replacement"
- "Easier than DOSBox"
- "Windows emulator Mac free"

### Legal Considerations
- **Fair use**: Comparative advertising is legal
- **Truthful claims**: All speed/cost comparisons must be accurate
- **No trademark issues**: Use competitor names factually, not confusingly

## Success Metrics

**Awareness**: Direct competitor comparisons in comments
**Conversion**: "Came here from DOSBox frustration" type feedback
**Market Share**: Users switching from paid alternatives
**Brand Positioning**: "RetroTick = simple, competitors = complex"

### A/B Testing
- **Aggressive vs Subtle**: Direct callouts vs implied comparisons
- **Feature vs Price**: Emphasize ease-of-use vs cost savings
- **Technical vs Emotional**: Logical benefits vs frustration relief
